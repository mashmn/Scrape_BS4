<p><b>Power Rangers</b> is an American entertainment and merchandising <a href="/wiki/Media_franchise" title="Media franchise">franchise</a> built around a live action superhero television series. Produced first by <a href="/wiki/Saban_Entertainment" title="Saban Entertainment">Saban Entertainment</a>, later by <a href="/wiki/BVS_Entertainment" class="mw-redirect" title="BVS Entertainment">BVS Entertainment</a>, and as of 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Power_Rangers&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> by <a href="/wiki/SCG_Power_Rangers" class="mw-redirect" title="SCG Power Rangers">SCG Power Rangers</a>, the television series takes much of its <a href="/wiki/Footage" title="Footage">footage</a> from the Japanese <a href="/wiki/Tokusatsu" title="Tokusatsu">tokusatsu</a> <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Sentai" title="Super Sentai">Super Sentai</a></i>, produced by <a href="/wiki/Toei_Company" title="Toei Company">Toei Company</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-1">[1]</a></sup> The first <i>Power Rangers</i> entry, <i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</a></i>, debuted on August 28, 1993, and helped launch the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Kids" title="Fox Kids">Fox Kids</a> programming block of the 1990s, during which it catapulted into <a href="/wiki/Popular_culture" title="Popular culture">popular culture</a> along with a line of <a href="/wiki/Action_figure" title="Action figure">action figures</a> and other toys by <a href="/wiki/Bandai" title="Bandai">Bandai</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2">[2]</a></sup> As of 2001<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Power_Rangers&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, the media franchise has generated over $6 billion in retail sales worldwide.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">[3]</a></sup></p>
<p>Despite initial <a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers#Reception_and_controversy" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">criticism</a> for its action violence targeted child audiences, the franchise has continued, and as of 2017<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Power_Rangers&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> the show consists of 24 television seasons of 20 different themed series and two theatrical films, with a third film released in 2017. In 2010, <a href="/wiki/Haim_Saban" title="Haim Saban">Haim Saban</a>, creator of the series, regained ownership of the franchise after seven years under <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a>.</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Premise">Premise</span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Further information: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Power_Rangers_characters" title="List of Power Rangers characters">List of Power Rangers characters</a> and <a href="/wiki/List_of_Power_Rangers_villains" title="List of Power Rangers villains">List of Power Rangers villains</a></div>
<p>Since <i>Power Rangers</i> derives most of its footage from the <i><a href="/wiki/Super_Sentai" title="Super Sentai">Super Sentai</a></i> series, it features many <a href="/wiki/Super_Sentai#Common_elements" title="Super Sentai">hallmarks</a> that distinguish it from other superhero series. Each series revolves around a team of youths recruited and trained by a mentor to morph into the eponymous Power Rangers, able to utilize special powers and pilot immense assault machines, called Zords, to overcome the periodic antagonists. In the original series <i>Mighty Morphin</i>, the wizard <a href="/wiki/Zordon" title="Zordon">Zordon</a> recruits "<a href="/wiki/Teenagers" class="mw-redirect" title="Teenagers">teenagers</a> with attitude" against <a href="/wiki/Rita_Repulsa" title="Rita Repulsa">Rita Repulsa</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">[4]</a></sup></p>
<p>When "morphed," the rangers become powerful <a href="/wiki/Superhero" title="Superhero">superheroes</a> wearing color-coded skin-tight <a href="/wiki/Spandex" title="Spandex">spandex</a> suits and helmets with <a href="/wiki/Opacity_(optics)" title="Opacity (optics)">opaque</a> <a href="/wiki/Visor" title="Visor">visors</a>; identical except in individual rangers' color and helmet design. Morphed Rangers generally possess enhanced strength, durability, agility and combat prowess. Some possess superhuman or <a href="/wiki/Psychic" title="Psychic">psychic</a> abilities such as super-speed, element manipulation, <a href="/wiki/Extra-sensory_perception" class="mw-redirect" title="Extra-sensory perception">extra-sensory perception</a> or <a href="/wiki/Invisibility" title="Invisibility">invisibility</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">[5]</a></sup> In addition, each individual ranger has a unique <a href="/wiki/Weapon" title="Weapon">weapon</a>, as well as common weaponry used for ground fighting.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">[note 1]</a></sup> When enemies grow to incredible size (as nearly all do), Rangers utilize individual Zords that combine into a larger Megazord.</p>
<p>Rangers teams operate in teams of five or three, with more Rangers joining the team later. Each team of Rangers, with a few exceptions, obeys a general set of conventions, outlined at the beginning of <i>Mighty Morphin</i> and implied by mentors throughout many of the other series: Power Rangers may not use their Ranger powers for personal gain or for escalating a fight (unless the enemy does so), nor may the Power Rangers disclose their identities to the general public.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">[note 2]</a></sup> The penalty for disobeying these rules, in <i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</a></i>, is the loss of their power.</p>
<p>As in <i>Super Sentai</i>, the color palette of each <i>Power Rangers</i> team changes every series.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">[note 3]</a></sup> Only Red and Blue appear in every Ranger team, while a Yellow Ranger has been present in every season except <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_Dino_Charge" title="Power Rangers Dino Charge">Power Rangers Dino Charge</a></i>. Other colors and designations also appear throughout the series.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">[note 4]</a></sup> A Rangers' color designation also influences their wardrobe throughout the series: civilian clothing often matches Ranger color.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">[note 5]</a></sup></p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Adapting_the_Super_Sentai_series">Adapting the <i>Super Sentai</i> series</span></h3>
<table class="plainlinks metadata ambox mbox-small-left ambox-content" role="presentation">

<p>Production of <i>Power Rangers</i> episodes involves extensive localization of and revision of original <i>Super Sentai</i> source material in order to incorporate American culture and conform to American television standards. Rather than making an English <a href="/wiki/Dubbing_(filmmaking)" title="Dubbing (filmmaking)">dub</a> or <a href="/wiki/Translation" title="Translation">translation</a> of the Japanese footage, <i>Power Rangers</i> programs consist of scenes featuring <a href="/wiki/English_Language" class="mw-redirect" title="English Language">English-speaking</a> actors (either from the <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a>, <a href="/wiki/Canada" title="Canada">Canada</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>, or the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a>) spliced with scenes featuring either Japanese actors dubbed into English or the action scenes from the Super Sentai Series featuring the Rangers fighting <a href="/wiki/Kaiju" title="Kaiju">monsters</a> or the <a href="/wiki/Mecha" title="Mecha">giant robot</a> (Zord and Megazord) battles with English dubbing. In some series, original fight scenes are filmed to incorporate characters or items unique to the <i>Power Rangers</i> production.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">[6]</a></sup> Like many of <a href="/wiki/Saban_Entertainment" title="Saban Entertainment">Saban Entertainment</a> previous ventures in <a href="/wiki/Language_localisation" title="Language localisation">localizing</a> Japanese television for a <a href="/wiki/Western_culture" title="Western culture">Western</a> audience, the plot, character names, and other names usually differ greatly from the source footage, though a few seasons have stayed close to the story of the original Super Sentai season.</p>
<p>Along with adapting the villains from the Super Sentai counterparts, most <i>Power Rangers</i> series also feature villains with no Sentai counterpart. Generally, the primary antagonist of a <i>Power Rangers</i> series (for example, Lord Zedd, Divatox, etc) are not adapted from the Sentai. Exceptions to this includes <i>Mighty Morphin</i>, <i>Zeo</i>, <i>Lightspeed Rescue</i> and a few others which only use villains adapted from the Japanese shows.</p>
<p>The series that began the <a href="/wiki/Media_franchise" title="Media franchise">franchise</a>, <i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</a></i> (an American adaptation of the 1992 Japanese <a href="/wiki/Super_Sentai" title="Super Sentai">Super Sentai</a> Series, <i><a href="/wiki/Ky%C5%8Dry%C5%AB_Sentai_Zyuranger" title="Kyōryū Sentai Zyuranger">Kyōryū Sentai Zyuranger</a></i>), began broadcasting as part of the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Kids" title="Fox Kids">Fox Kids</a> block of programing that aired on the <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">FOX</a> network. It lasted for three seasons (from 1993 to 1996).<sup id="cite_ref-MMPR2010_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MMPR2010-12">[7]</a></sup></p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Broadcast_history">Broadcast history</span></h3>
<div class="thumb tright">
<div class="thumbinner" style="width:252px;"><a href="/wiki/File:Power_rangers.png" class="image"><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/01/Power_rangers.png/250px-Power_rangers.png" width="250" height="125" class="thumbimage" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/01/Power_rangers.png/375px-Power_rangers.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/0/01/Power_rangers.png/500px-Power_rangers.png 2x" data-file-width="565" data-file-height="283" /></a>
<div class="thumbcaption">

The Saban Entertainment run of the franchise—beginning with <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_in_Space" title="Power Rangers in Space">Power Rangers in Space</a></i>—used this version of the <i>Power Rangers</i> logo (1998-2001)</div>

<p><a href="/wiki/Saban_Entertainment" title="Saban Entertainment">Saban Entertainment</a> distributed the <i>Power Rangers</i> series from 1993 until the end of 2001, and Fox broadcast it until the <a href="/wiki/Autumn" title="Autumn">fall</a> of 2002. <a href="/wiki/The_Walt_Disney_Company" title="The Walt Disney Company">The Walt Disney Company</a> purchased the franchise as part of a buyout that took place in 2001.<sup id="cite_ref-MMPR2010_12-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MMPR2010-12">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-HaimSaban_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HaimSaban-13">[8]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">[9]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-buyout_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buyout-15">[10]</a></sup> This resulted in Fox Family Worldwide becoming <a href="/wiki/ABC_Family_Worldwide_Inc." class="mw-redirect" title="ABC Family Worldwide Inc.">ABC Family Worldwide Inc.</a><sup id="cite_ref-buyout_15-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buyout-15">[10]</a></sup> This buyout also saw Saban Entertainment becoming BVS Entertainment in 2002, from <a href="/wiki/News_Corporation" title="News Corporation">News Corporation</a>, Fox's parent company, and <a href="/wiki/Haim_Saban" title="Haim Saban">Haim Saban</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-buyout_15-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buyout-15">[10]</a></sup> The show continued to air on Fox until the company replaced its <a href="/wiki/Fox_Kids" title="Fox Kids">Fox Kids</a> package with "FoxBox" in the United States. Since September 2002, all <i>Power Rangers</i> shows had aired on various Disney-owned networks (<a href="/wiki/ABC_Kids_(United_States)" title="ABC Kids (United States)">ABC Kids</a>, <a href="/wiki/Toon_Disney" title="Toon Disney">Toon Disney</a> and <a href="/wiki/Jetix" title="Jetix">Jetix</a> channels worldwide).<sup id="cite_ref-MMPR2010_12-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MMPR2010-12">[7]</a></sup> When <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_Wild_Force" title="Power Rangers Wild Force">Wild Force</a></i> ended, Disney moved production of the franchise from Los Angeles to New Zealand. This resulted in the closure of MMPR Productions and the dismissal of many members of the production. From <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_Ninja_Storm" title="Power Rangers Ninja Storm">Ninja Storm</a></i> to date, <i>Power Rangers</i> is produced in New Zealand. ABC Family, another Disney-owned network, also used to air <i>Power Rangers</i> until it did away with its Jetix timeslot after August 31, 2006. On February 12, 2009, Toon Disney ended in the wake of <a href="/wiki/Disney_XD" title="Disney XD">Disney XD</a>, ending cable airings of <i>Power Rangers</i> in certain areas of the United States. Several ABC affiliate broadcasting groups declined to air most of the <i>Power Rangers</i> series since 2006 due to the lack of FCC-compliant <a href="/wiki/E/I" title="E/I">educational and informational content</a> in the programs.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">[11]</a></sup></p>
<p>The Saban era seasons, starting with <i>In Space</i> have went under the "Saban's Power Rangers" moniker, up until <i>Time Force</i>. Since the re-acquisition of <i>Power Rangers</i> by Saban in 2010, this practice has continued once again starting with <i>Samurai</i>.</p>
<p>Starting in 2005, up until 2007, during its run on Jetix, <i>Power Rangers</i> reruns were aired under the moniker <i>Power Rangers Generations</i>, showcasing select episodes from <i>Mighty Morphin</i> through <i>Dino Thunder</i>.</p>
<p>An article in <i><a href="/wiki/The_New_Zealand_Herald" title="The New Zealand Herald">The New Zealand Herald</a></i> published on March 7, 2009 identified <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_RPM" title="Power Rangers RPM">Power Rangers RPM</a></i> as the last season of the <i>Power Rangers</i> run. Production manager Sally Campbell stated in an interview, "...at this stage we will not be shooting another season."<sup id="cite_ref-lightsout_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lightsout-17">[12]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">[13]</a></sup> A September 1, 2009, revision to <i><a href="/wiki/Disney_A_to_Z:_The_Official_Encyclopedia" class="mw-redirect" title="Disney A to Z: The Official Encyclopedia">Disney A to Z: The Official Encyclopedia</a></i> by Disney's head archivist <a href="/wiki/Dave_Smith_(archivist)" title="Dave Smith (archivist)">Dave Smith</a> states that "production of new episodes [of <i>Power Rangers</i>] ceased in 2009".<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">[14]</a></sup> Production of <i>Power Rangers</i> ceased and the last<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Power_Rangers&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup> series by BVS Entertainment, <i>RPM</i>, ended on December 26, 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-lightsout_17-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-lightsout-17">[12]</a></sup></p>
<p>On October 1, 2009, Bandai released a press release that Disney would re-broadcast <i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</a></i> starting in January 2010 on <a href="/wiki/ABC_Kids_(United_States)" title="ABC Kids (United States)">ABC Kids</a> in lieu of a new series utilizing footage from the <a href="/wiki/Samurai_Sentai_Shinkenger" title="Samurai Sentai Shinkenger">2009 <i>Super Sentai</i> television series</a>. A new toy line accompanied the series and appeared in stores in the later part of 2009.<sup id="cite_ref-MMPR2010_12-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MMPR2010-12">[7]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Bandai_MMPR_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Bandai_MMPR-20">[15]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-businesswire_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-businesswire-21">[16]</a></sup> ABC's over-the air telecasts ended on August 28, 2010, and turned the hour back to affiliates.</p>
<p>On May 12, 2010, <a href="/wiki/Haim_Saban" title="Haim Saban">Haim Saban</a> bought back the <i>Power Rangers</i> franchise from Disney for $43 million and announced plans to produce a new season of the television series.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">[17]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-buy_back_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buy_back-23">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">[19]</a></sup> The eighteenth season, <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_Samurai" title="Power Rangers Samurai">Samurai</a></i>, began airing on <a href="/wiki/Nickelodeon" title="Nickelodeon">Nickelodeon</a> on February 7, 2011,<sup id="cite_ref-buy_back_23-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-buy_back-23">[18]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-cast_and_premiere_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cast_and_premiere-25">[20]</a></sup> with the previous episodes beginning rebroadcast on <a href="/wiki/Nicktoons_(TV_channel)" title="Nicktoons (TV channel)">Nicktoons</a> later that year.<sup id="cite_ref-cast_and_premiere_25-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cast_and_premiere-25">[20]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">[21]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">[22]</a></sup> It was also announced that Saban plans to make a new Power Rangers movie.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">[23]</a></sup></p>
<p>On July 2, 2012, it was announced that <a href="/wiki/Saban_Capital_Group" title="Saban Capital Group">Saban Brands</a> would launch a <a href="/wiki/Saturday_morning_cartoon" class="mw-redirect" title="Saturday morning cartoon">Saturday morning cartoon</a> block on <a href="/wiki/The_CW_Television_Network" class="mw-redirect" title="The CW Television Network">The CW</a>, called <a href="/wiki/Vortexx" title="Vortexx">Vortexx</a>, on August 25, 2012 that would air <i>Power Rangers Lost Galaxy</i>.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">[24]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">[25]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">[26]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">[27]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">[28]</a></sup> To commemorate the series' 20th anniversary, Nickelodeon began airing <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_Megaforce" title="Power Rangers Megaforce">Power Rangers Megaforce</a></i> on February 2, 2013, featuring all of the past rangers from the series' 20-year history. On October 1, 2013, Saban Brands announced that it has extended agreements with Nickelodeon and Bandai America Incorporated through 2016 for its globally recognized Power Rangers franchise.<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">[29]</a></sup> In January 2016, Saban and Nickelodeon extended their broadcast partnership through 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">[30]</a></sup></p>
<p><a href="/wiki/The_90s_Are_All_That" class="mw-redirect" title="The 90s Are All That">The 90s Are All That</a> aired <i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</a></i> part of Mighty Morphin Weekend in 2013.</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Television_series">Television series</span></h2>
<div role="note" class="hatnote">Main article: <a href="/wiki/List_of_Power_Rangers_episodes" title="List of Power Rangers episodes">List of Power Rangers episodes</a></div>
<p>The first series <i>Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</i> through <i>In Space</i> followed a <a href="/wiki/Story_arc" title="Story arc">story arc</a> with a gradually changing cast and characters over six years. Beginning with <i>Lost Galaxy</i>, although it had ties with the previous story arc, each <i>Power Rangers</i> series had its own self-contained <a href="/wiki/Plot_(narrative)" title="Plot (narrative)">storylines</a>, independent of previous series. <a href="/wiki/Fictional_crossover" class="mw-redirect" title="Fictional crossover">Crossover</a> episodes between different series featuring rangers, villains, and other characters from past seasons also began with <i>Lost Galaxy</i>, with a few exceptions.</p>

<li><span id="Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers"></span><i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers</a></i> (1993–1996)
<ul>
<li>Season 1: When the evil witch <a href="/wiki/Rita_Repulsa" title="Rita Repulsa">Rita Repulsa</a> and her minions <a href="/wiki/Goldar" class="mw-redirect" title="Goldar">Goldar</a>, Squatt, Baboo, and Finster, are freed from their imprisonment on the Moon, the wizard <a href="/wiki/Zordon" title="Zordon">Zordon</a>, with the help of his assistant <a href="/wiki/Alpha_5_(Power_Rangers)" class="mw-redirect" title="Alpha 5 (Power Rangers)">Alpha 5</a>, enlists five teenagers—<a href="/wiki/Jason_Lee_Scott" title="Jason Lee Scott">Jason Lee Scott</a>, <a href="/wiki/Trini_Kwan" title="Trini Kwan">Trini Kwan</a>, <a href="/wiki/Billy_Cranston" title="Billy Cranston">Billy Cranston</a>, <a href="/wiki/Kimberly_Hart" title="Kimberly Hart">Kimberly Hart</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Zack_Taylor" title="Zack Taylor">Zack Taylor</a>—to become the Mighty Morphin Power Rangers to battle Rita's invasion of the Earth, using their Power Coins and Dinozords to combat her monsters. Rita soon creates her own evil Power Ranger by brainwashing recent transfer student <a href="/wiki/Tommy_Oliver" title="Tommy Oliver">Tommy Oliver</a>, but the Power Rangers are able to free him from her control and he joins their side. This season uses footage from <i><a href="/wiki/Ky%C5%8Dryu_Sentai_Zyuranger" class="mw-redirect" title="Kyōryu Sentai Zyuranger">Kyōryu Sentai Zyuranger</a></i>.</li>
<li>Season 2: Rita's superior <a href="/wiki/Lord_Zedd" class="mw-redirect" title="Lord Zedd">Lord Zedd</a> returns to the Earth to take over for Rita's incompetence, imprisoning her once more. His new monsters prove too powerful for the Dinozords, leading Zordon to empower them into the Thunderzords. Jason, Trini, and Zack leave to join a youth conference in Switzerland, and are replaced by <a href="/wiki/Rocky_DeSantos" title="Rocky DeSantos">Rocky DeSantos</a>, <a href="/wiki/Aisha_Campbell" title="Aisha Campbell">Aisha Campbell</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Adam_Park" title="Adam Park">Adam Park</a>, respectively, and Rita returns and marries Lord Zedd. <li><i><a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers:_The_Movie" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers: The Movie">Mighty Morphin Power Rangers: The Movie</a></i> (1995): When the evil wizard <a href="/wiki/Ivan_Ooze" class="mw-redirect" title="Ivan Ooze">Ivan Ooze</a> is freed by construction work, he takes revenge against Zordon for imprisoning him. As his powers are too strong for the Power Rangers, Alpha 5 uses the last of the Command Center's power to send them to the planet Phaedos to learn the ways of the Ninjetti from the warrior Dulcea to stop Ooze before he can conquer the universe. The movie, which does not fall into the canon of the TV series, drew inspiration from <i><a href="/wiki/Ninja_Sentai_Kakuranger" title="Ninja Sentai Kakuranger">Ninja Sentai Kakuranger</a></i>.</li>

The Power Rangers seek out <a href="/wiki/Ninjor_(Power_Rangers)" class="mw-redirect" title="Ninjor (Power Rangers)">Ninjor</a>, creator of the Power Coins, for new Power Coins giving them new ninja powers and the Ninjazords, and later the Shogunzords. Rita also enchants a new Australian exchange student <a href="/wiki/Kat_Hillard" title="Kat Hillard">Kat Hillard</a> into slowly draining Kimberly of her Ranger powers, until the spell is broken; when Kimberly ultimately leaves to train for the Olympics, she entrusts Kat to be her replacement amongst the Power Rangers. This season uses footage from <i><a href="/wiki/Ninja_Sentai_Kakuranger" title="Ninja Sentai Kakuranger">Ninja Sentai Kakuranger</a></i>.</li>
</ul>
</li>

Zordon calls upon his proteges the Alien Rangers of Aquitar to protect the Earth from Master Vile's monsters and to help work on a machine that would return the child rangers to their normal ages. Unfortunately, Only Billy returns to normal as the machine which required the power coins as a power source is stolen by Rito &amp; Goldar allowing Rita &amp; Zedd to destroy the coins. The child rangers then travel throughout time to gather the fragments of the Zeo Crystal to bring time back to normal. During her journey, Aisha meets <a href="/wiki/Tanya_Sloan" title="Tanya Sloan">Tanya Sloan</a> and realizes that she can do more help to stop the sickness plaguing the wildlife and sends Tanya back with her Zeo Crystal. This series also uses footage from <i><a href="/wiki/Ninja_Sentai_Kakuranger" title="Ninja Sentai Kakuranger">Ninja Sentai Kakuranger</a></i>.</li>

Although Zordon reveals he is fine, he warns the Rangers that the <a href="/wiki/Machine_Empire" title="Machine Empire">Machine Empire</a> is planning on conquering the Earth, and the Rangers must use the power of the Zeo Crystal to become the Zeo Rangers to battle them. Billy, who used his scientific prowess to return to his original age during <i>Alien Rangers</i>, decides to work on the Zeo Zords and other machinery, and his presence in the destruction of the previous Command Center leaves him unable to receive the powers of the Gold Ranger, <a href="/wiki/Trey_of_Triforia" class="mw-redirect" title="Trey of Triforia">Trey of Triforia</a>, but Jason returns instead. Billy soon begins to age rapidly, a side effect From The Regenerator he used to return to his normal age, and leaves Earth to be healed on Aquitar. This season uses footage from <i><a href="/wiki/Ch%C5%8Driki_Sentai_Ohranger" class="mw-redirect" title="Chōriki Sentai Ohranger">Chōriki Sentai Ohranger</a></i>.</li>

They are later joined by Dana's long lost brother Ryan. This season uses footage from <i><a href="/wiki/Kyuukyuu_Sentai_GoGoFive" title="Kyuukyuu Sentai GoGoFive">Kyuukyuu Sentai GoGoFive</a></i>.</li>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Distribution">Distribution</span></h2>
<p><i>Power Rangers</i> has long had success in international markets and continues to air in many countries, with the exception of New Zealand, where the series filming takes place as of 2009<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Power_Rangers&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>. As of 2006, <i>Power Rangers</i> aired at least 65 times a week in more than 40 worldwide markets.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">[37]</a></sup> Many markets carry or have carried the series on their respective <a href="/wiki/Fox_Broadcasting_Company" title="Fox Broadcasting Company">Fox</a> or later <a href="/wiki/Jetix" title="Jetix">Jetix</a>/<a href="/wiki/Disney_XD" title="Disney XD">Disney XD</a> channels or have syndicated the program on regional children's channels or blocks, either dubbed into the local language or broadcast in the original English. Since the 2010 acquisition by Saban Brands, international television distribution rights for Power Rangers have been managed by <a href="/wiki/MarVista_Entertainment" title="MarVista Entertainment">MarVista Entertainment</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">[38]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">[39]</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">[40]</a></sup></p>
<p>Broadcast in East Asian territories has been treated differently from in other international markets due to the prevalence and familiarity of 'the <i>Super Sentai</i> brand originating in Japan. <i>Power Rangers</i> was briefly banned in Malaysia for supposedly encouraging the use of drugs because it contained the word "Morphin'" in its title, which could be associated with <a href="/wiki/Morphine" title="Morphine">morphine</a>. The show eventually aired without the offending word.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">[41]</a></sup> In Japan, many <i>Power Rangers</i> television seasons and movies were dubbed into Japanese for television and video with the voice actors often pulled from past <i>Super Sentai</i> casts, leading to the English-dubbed <a href="/wiki/Stage_combat" title="Stage combat">action sequences</a> being "re-dubbed" or "restored" back to Japanese as well. <i>Power Rangers Mystic Force</i> is the latest season to be broadcast in Japan on <a href="/w/index.php?title=Toei_Channel&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Toei Channel (page does not exist)">Toei Channel</a> in January 2014, with the <i>Magiranger</i> cast voicing their counterparts. After broadcast of <i>Power Rangers</i> ended in South Korea with <i>Wild Force</i>, Bandai of Korea started airing <a href="/wiki/Super_Sentai#South_Korea" title="Super Sentai">dubbed <i>Super Sentai</i> series</a> under the <i>파워레인저</i> <i>(Power Ranger)</i> brand on <a href="/w/index.php?title=JEI_TV&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="JEI TV (page does not exist)">JEI TV</a>. Some seasons of <i>Super Sentai</i> broadcast in South Korea have similarly named titles as their American counterparts, such as <i>Power Ranger Dino Thunder</i><sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">[42]</a></sup> for <i>Abaranger</i> in 2007 and <i>Power Ranger S.P.D.</i><sup id="cite_ref-48" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-48">[43]</a></sup> in place of <i>Dekaranger</i>.</p>

In 2012, Mighty Morphin Power Rangers Season 1 volumes 1-2 were released on iTunes to coincide with the DVD releases. As of February 2013, all 3 seasons of <i>MMPR</i> were released on iTunes.</p>
<p>On June 15, 2011, all episodes of Power Rangers from <a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers_(season_1)" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers (season 1)"><i>Mighty Morphin Power Rangers Season 1</i></a> to <a href="/wiki/Mighty_Morphin_Power_Rangers_(re-version)" title="Mighty Morphin Power Rangers (re-version)"><i>Mighty Morphin Power Rangers re-version</i></a> were made available for instant streaming on Netflix.<sup id="cite_ref-68" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-68">[63]</a></sup> As of 2015, all seasons through <i>Dino Charge</i> have been made available on Netflix</p>
<p>On March 12, 2012, <a href="/wiki/Shout!_Factory" title="Shout! Factory">Shout! Factory</a> announced a home video distribution deal with Saban, which includes the first 17 series of Power Rangers. Shout! Factory released the first seven seasons on DVD in August 2012,<sup id="cite_ref-69" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-69">[64]</a></sup> seasons 8-12 on November 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-70" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-70">[65]</a></sup> a 20-year collection on December 2013,<sup id="cite_ref-71" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-71">[66]</a></sup> and seasons 13-17 on April 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-72" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-72">[67]</a></sup></p>
<p>On March 22, 2012 <a href="/wiki/Lionsgate_Home_Entertainment" title="Lionsgate Home Entertainment">Lionsgate Home Entertainment</a> reached a home media distribution deal with Saban to release <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_Samurai" title="Power Rangers Samurai">Power Rangers Samurai</a></i> to DVD and Blu-ray.<sup id="cite_ref-73" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-73">[68]</a></sup></p>
<p>As of April 2015<sup class="plainlinks noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="//en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Power_Rangers&amp;action=edit">[update]</a></sup>, all series through <i>Super Megaforce</i> are available on the <a href="/wiki/ITunes_Store" title="ITunes Store">iTunes Store</a>.</p>

<li id="cite_note-6"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-6">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">As the series progresses, one or more of the Rangers will usually receive <a href="/wiki/Motorcycle" title="Motorcycle">motorcycles</a> for long-distance travel, as well as individual Zords. In many series, a Ranger is also given additional Zords or weapons. In some cases, one Ranger may receive something that other Rangers do not; an example is the <a href="/wiki/Battlizer" class="mw-redirect" title="Battlizer">Battlizer</a> given to the Red Ranger of each series since <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_in_Space" title="Power Rangers in Space">Power Rangers in Space</a></i> (until <i><a href="/wiki/Power_Rangers_Operation_Overdrive" title="Power Rangers Operation Overdrive">Operation Overdrive</a></i>).</span></li>

<li id="cite_note-9"><span class="mw-cite-backlink"><b><a href="#cite_ref-9">^</a></b></span> <span class="reference-text">Other color designations include <a href="/wiki/Metal" title="Metal">metallic</a> colors, violet, and "Shadow", as well as protagonists who have powers and costumes similar to those of the Rangers but are not called "Power Rangers", such as the <a href="/wiki/Blue_Senturion" class="mw-redirect" title="Blue Senturion">Blue Senturion</a> and <a href="/wiki/Koragg_the_Knight_Wolf" class="mw-redirect" title="Koragg the Knight Wolf">Koragg the Knight Wolf</a>.</span></li>
